Disparities in Women's Health

We want to encourage women to go to the docotor more often and sooner, and to be more insistent at being taken seriously

Compared with men, women with heart disease more likely to report more treatment and care disparities, Johns Hopkins Medicine 2018 Link to website

Women and pain: Disparities in experience and treatment, Harvard Health Publishing 2017

Link to website

How doctors take women's pain less seriously, The Atlantic 2015

A story of a trip to the Emergency Room and not being taken seriously about her pain. Men wait an average of 49 minute sin pain while women wait 65 minutes for the same thing, the woman in the story had to wait almost 2 hours. Link to website

Sex Differences in Reported Pain Across 11,000 Patients Captured in Electronic Medical Records

A scientific research study that looked at medical records and found that women report higher pain intensity for muscuoloskeletal diseases. Journal of Pain, 2013. Link to website

Researcher says women less likely to get painkillers

Doctors didn't take women's complaints about pain after surgery seriously. Some women were given sedatives instead of painkillers but none of the men who had the same surgery were given sedatives. Link to website

Google's speech recognition has a gender bias

Automated speech recognition systems make more errors for black speakers than white speakers. There's a 70% chance transcriptions for a man will be more accurate than for a woman. Link to website

Siri is sexist, Forbes 2011

Link to website

Validity of Consumer-Based Physical Activity Monitors for Specific Acitivty Types, 2016

A scientific study looking at the 12 most common fitness monitors, like smart watches. They found that the fitness monitors underestimated steps taken and calories burned by users when performing houseowkr. Housework is a female-associated task that isn't consiered enough in the development of fitness monitors. Link to website

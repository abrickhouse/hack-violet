Virginia Tech Resources

For now, our project focuses on the women of the Virginia Tech community, as that is where HackViolet is based out of. However, in the future, we strive to implement more region specific aids.

Schiffert Health Center, Women's Clinic

The women's clinic provides breast exams, Pap tests, pelvic exams, pregnancy tests and counseling, STI screening and treatments, contraceptive perscriptions, and more. They also have a lot of information on specific contraceptives, specific STIs, and other infections like yeast infections and UTIs. Link to website

Women's Center at Virginia Tech

The Women's Center focuses on gender equity. They have a podcast covering topics like leadership, wellbieng, and healthy relationships from a gender and identity-based perspective. They also provide workshops including salary negotiation and bystander training to end sexual violence. They host special events for awareness of topics relevant to women, like Women's History Month and Domestic Violence Awareness Month. They provide support for Title IX cases, including seuxal assaul and stalking. For students, they have opportunities to volunteer, do a work-study, or complete an internship. Link to website

Hokie Wellness Sexual Health Resources

Hokie Wellness provides workshops on topics like body confidence, STD/STI information, and Helping Friends in Distress. They also provide coupons for condoms, as well as Plan B and other emergency contraceptives. They do testing for HIV, STDs, and STIs. Students can receive free HIV, Hep C, and syphilis testing. Link to website

Cook Counseling Center

The Cook Counseling Center supports both undergraduate and graduate students. They provide individual, couples, or group therapy. They also do outreach to student clubs and departments. Link to website

Body Matters Week

This week-long series of events usually occurs in April and is focused on nutrition, self acceptance, and disordered eating awareness and treatment. Link to website

Healthy Eating and Assessment Referral Team (HEART)

HEART provides support and referral services to students at Virginia Tech related to healthy eating. The team consists of medical providers, conselors, registered nurses, a dietitian, a fitness specialist, and a case manager. Link to website
